General


During this project I had to take references from other website and webpages. While taking references, i only implemented the design and layout of that page into mine. Throught the project (entire website), I did not copy a single line from source code of other sites. However there were some instances where I had to look up tags, their attributes and how they work from other site (mostly w3Schools ).

With the help of w3schools I implemented text animation in my home page. I also learned a little bit about how to use flex and its property which was a major help in this project as i've used that to provide structure to the pages. For my contact page which has a form, I had to use javascript for validation and I went to w3Schools to learn about js syntax (mostly how DOM works). I also implemented the progress-bar in cv page from w3 schools.

Home Page


I took Sean O' Connor's site as showcased at college in for geek's article about portfolio websites. as a reference for my home page and implemented the layout and design to my own. Image and text placement on both of our Home pages are similar. The navigation bar and footer section were however referenced from another source which is explained in Navigation & Footer .

CV Page


I Found this image of CV Template on google while searching for "designs for CV". This layout originally belongs to www.mycvtemplates.com . I implemented the basics overally layout of this image into my cv page but the contents inside of cv are not similar to reference image but are heavily modified and changed.

Blog and Research Page

I came across this blog site while researching for a simplistic and minimalistic design. I took reference for my blog page from at home with natalie. I took the image, date, title and content part from the reference site and used it as a design for my site. Initially I wanted an image on the left and blog content on the right being arranged side to side (card) but, while implementing it during coding phase, I accidentally generated the current layout (I kept image inside of paragraph tag instead of making a seperate div for it). After accidentally generating something else, I decided to stick with it.

For the navigation card on the left of blog and research page, I took reference from w3schools which has a inside-page navigation on its left side. I recycled the code from the cv page (of my site) into a two card layout- left card for within-page navigation and right card for content (of blog and research). The difference in the reference(image) and my research page is that i have a certain gap between left card and right card and have added a logo to menu items.

Contact Page


The design, layout of my contact page was heavily influenced by the design posted in UI cookies website. To make my contact page as close to the reference, I had to take help of w3 schools. From w3 schools, I looked up how to style textbox, buttons and edit placeholders (I did not know pseudo elements existed) and designed accordingly. Apart from the color theme and font, my contact page looks similar to the reference.

Navigation & Footer


The design and Layout of my navigation bar was heavily influenced by the design of Vikalp Kaushik which was posted on Ux planet's website . Just like the reference, I went with minimalistic navbar with simple font and transparent background. However, unlike the reference, I did not use and image avatar on my navbar but instead replaced that with an image logo which I made using Adobe Illustrator.


The design and Layout of my footer was influenced by the footer of Random-ize website. Instead of Youtube, Twitter and Facebook icons in their footer, I used Facebook, Gmail and Twitter icons. The text and icons in the reference footer is on the same line but on my site, I placed logo and text on seperate lines (seperate div). The buttons on referenced footer icons and my footer icons serve the same purpose and have similar workings , they both open respective links in new tab.
